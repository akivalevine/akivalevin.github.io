In this photo we are of course admiring the view while walking across.

Equadorian Amazon

One of the days we were at the lodge, we visited this foreign Amazon tribe. They are so distant from the outside world, that they visit Coca, Equador once a year at most, and Quito, once in their lifetime! It was truly a memorable experience, where we got to learn about their cultures and many foods they eat. In this photo, I am trying a Yucca like plant.

Equadorian Amazon

While we were at the tribe, the native people showed us the larvaes they eat. Though we did not eat it, because they weren't kosher and it was disgusting, our tour guide did. This is a video of him eating the bug.

Equadorian Amazon

On lake Pilchocha, we went Piranha fishing and my dad caught a large Piranha. We later cooked it up with the help of the tour guide since it was kosher, and then they took the jaw out to keep as a souvenir of the front and bottom set of teeth. As of today, we still have it!

Equadorian Amazon
